The firm’s experience spans bulk vessel shipments, containerised cargoes and cross-border physical trade across multiple origins and destinations.

Our core strength lies in our ability to translate contractual terms into smooth physical execution — ensuring that quality parameters, delivery terms, inspection procedures and documentation requirements are clearly aligned between buyers and sellers.

India Edible Oil Market — Supply Chain, Seasonality & Trade Flow

1. India’s Edible Oil Demand Structure

India’s edible oil market is structurally import-dependent. Annual consumption is estimated at 23–25 million metric tonnes, whereas domestic oilseed production supplies only a portion of this demand. The balance requirement is fulfilled through imports.

This import dependency is not temporary. It is driven by population size, food habits, limited land availability for oilseeds and yield variability due to monsoon dependence.

2. Primary Imported Oils & Origins

India imports multiple edible oils from diverse origins. Each oil has a distinct origin pattern and seasonality.

  • Palm Oil — Indonesia, Malaysia
  • Soybean Oil — Argentina, Brazil
  • Sunflower Oil — Ukraine, Russia, Black Sea region

Vessel arrivals depend on harvest cycles, export availability, freight markets and geopolitical factors.

3. Seasonal Import Pattern (Month-wise)

India’s edible oil imports follow a seasonal pattern rather than uniform monthly distribution.

  • October – January: Higher imports (festive demand, pipeline refill)
  • February – April: Stable imports
  • May – July: Slight moderation due to inventory drawdown
  • August – September: Pre-festival buildup begins

Brokers and importers plan vessel arrivals based on these demand cycles to avoid congestion and excess inventory.

4. Annual Import Volumes — Indicative

On an annual basis, India imports approximately 15–16 million metric tonnes of edible oils. Palm oil contributes the largest share, followed by soybean and sunflower oils.

Import volumes fluctuate year-to-year based on global supply, freight costs, domestic price controls and policy interventions.

5. Port-wise Import Distribution (Indicative)

India’s edible oil imports are concentrated across a few strategic ports. Western coast ports dominate due to proximity to origins and refinery clusters.

6. Stock Holding & Inventory Logic

Edible oil stocks in India are held across multiple levels:

  • Port-side tank farms
  • Refinery storage tanks
  • Inland depots & distribution hubs

Typical national stock coverage ranges between 30–45 days, depending on import pipeline, demand season and price outlook.
