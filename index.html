Unlike proprietary trading firms, KNG Commodities LLP does not engage in speculative positions, inventory holding, physical storage or logistics ownership. This ensures complete neutrality in every transaction and allows the firm to align its interests equally with both counterparties involved in a trade.

Over the years, the firm has worked with a wide spectrum of market participants including domestic refiners, processors, importers, exporters and multinational trading houses. KNG Commodities LLP has actively facilitated trade with organisations such as Viterra India, Bunge India, Adani Group, Tata Group and COFCO International, along with several established regional and local market participants. The firm’s experience spans bulk vessel shipments, containerised cargoes and cross-border physical trade across multiple origins and destinations.

Our core strength lies in our ability to translate contractual terms into smooth physical execution — ensuring that quality parameters, delivery terms, inspection procedures and documentation requirements are clearly aligned between buyers and sellers.

KNG Commodities LLP believes that a broker’s reputation is built not on volume claims, but on consistency, discretion and long-term credibility within the market.

India Edible Oil Market — Comprehensive Trade & Consumption Report

India is structurally dependent on imported edible oils. Despite being one of the world’s largest producers of oilseeds, domestic production remains insufficient to meet national demand. This imbalance has made India one of the largest importers of edible oils globally.

The country’s edible oil demand is driven by a combination of population growth, changing dietary habits, urbanisation and the rapid expansion of the food processing and hospitality sectors. As incomes rise and consumption patterns evolve, per-capita edible oil consumption has shown a steady upward trend.

Currently, India consumes approximately 23–25 million metric tonnes of edible oils annually, while domestic production contributes only a part of this requirement. The resulting supply gap is met through imports of palm oil, soybean oil, sunflower oil and other soft oils.

Market Interpretation & Risk Indicators

Import volumes have generally increased year-on-year, reflecting the structural nature of India’s dependence on global edible oil markets. However, certain years witnessed stress due to supply chain disruptions, geopolitical developments and freight market volatility.

● Growth years indicate stable supply chains, predictable freight availability and smooth execution across ports.

● Stress years reflect higher landed costs, port congestion, longer vessel turnaround times and increased execution risk.

These dynamics underline the importance of disciplined trade execution, contract clarity and experienced brokerage support in physical commodity markets.

Ports, Logistics & Inland Movement

India’s edible oil imports are concentrated around key western coast ports, which offer proximity to major producing origins and established refinery infrastructure. Efficient port operations are critical to controlling landed cost and ensuring timely availability of cargo.

  • Mundra Port — Major hub for bulk liquid cargoes with integrated tank farms.
  • Kandla (Deendayal Port) — Handles significant volumes for northern India.
  • JNPT / Nhava Sheva — Supports containerised edible oil movements.

Post-discharge, cargoes move through container yards and inland logistics hubs before reaching refineries and processing units. Coordination across these stages directly impacts demurrage exposure and supply continuity.

Processing, Refining & Domestic Consumption

Refineries and processing plants form the backbone of India’s edible oil ecosystem. Imported crude oils are refined, stabilised and blended to meet domestic quality and regulatory requirements before distribution.

With annual consumption exceeding domestic production, uninterrupted imports are essential to prevent shortages and price volatility. Processing capacity and logistics efficiency therefore play a strategic role in national food security.

Role of Brokerage in India’s Edible Oil Trade

Brokerage firms act as neutral facilitators in physical trade, ensuring that contractual obligations align with execution realities. This includes quality specifications, shipment windows, inspection protocols and documentation flow.

KNG Commodities LLP operates strictly as a broker-only intermediary and does not assume ownership, storage, logistics or financial exposure in any transaction.
